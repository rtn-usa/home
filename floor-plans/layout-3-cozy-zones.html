Layout 3: Cozy Zones - Design Notes

  • Master Bedroom: Queen bed between two nightstands with a sightline to the door (command position). Desk 1 is in the far corner creating a private workspace. Dog bed at the foot area so they can sleep near you. The desk faces the room, not the wall - better for focus and energy per feng shui.
  • 2nd Bedroom (Den/Lounge/Guest): The Joybird Carin sofa moves here as the centerpiece - it pulls out to a full guest bed when needed. TV and media console on the opposite wall. Bookshelf and side table complete a cozy reading/watching nook. Dog lounge area between sofa and TV for maximum togetherness. IKEA PAX along the upper wall.
  • Living / Dining: This becomes a true hybrid zone. A built-in bench seat along the angled west wall provides seating that doubles for dining, lounging, or extra workspace. Drop-leaf dining table near the kitchen expands for meals, folds down for space. Two floor poufs add flexible seating. Desk 2 tucked beside the dining area creates a compact work nook. Floating shelves on the wall add storage without floor footprint.
  • Multi-functional Design: The bench + poufs + drop-leaf table can be reconfigured: dinner party (table open, bench + chairs + poufs), work mode (one person at desk, one at bench with laptop), movie night (poufs pulled to den), dog hangout (dogs on bench or poufs).
  • Feng Shui Five Elements: Wood (plants in corners), Water (mirror at entry to reflect energy), Earth (poufs, natural textiles), Fire (warm-toned lighting), Metal (floating shelves, desk frames). Entry PAX keeps the entryway organized - first impressions matter for chi.
  • Dog-Friendly: Two dedicated dog zones (master + den), bench seat they can hop on, open floor paths for zoomies between rooms.
