Layout 2: Open Flow - Design Notes

  • Master Bedroom: Queen bed centered on the west wall in command position. Two nightstands for balance. Dresser near the door for additional storage. Dog bed in the quiet corner - dogs can see you but have their own retreat space.
  • 2nd Bedroom (Dedicated Office + Guest): Both desks side-by-side along the south wall - a proper two-person studio office. Murphy bed folds down from the west wall when guests arrive, clearing the desk area. IKEA PAX along the north wall. Filing cabinet for office storage.
  • Living / Dining: The Carin sofa acts as a room divider with its back to the entry, creating distinct zones. A console table behind the sofa serves as a landing surface and visual barrier. TV wall-mounted on the dividing wall. Bar-height dining table along the kitchen wall with 4 stools - great for casual meals and doubles as extra workspace.
  • Multi-functional Seating: An accent chair beside the sofa can be pulled to dining or turned for conversation. The bar stools do double duty for kitchen prep and dining.
  • Dog Space: Two spots - a primary lounge beside the sofa in the living room, and a bed in the master bedroom corner.
  • Feng Shui Principles: Sofa has solid wall behind it (via console table). Clear sightlines from entry. The bar table creates a gathering point that doesn't block energy flow. Murphy bed keeps the office energy clear when not in guest mode.
